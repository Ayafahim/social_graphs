Eric Ethridge is a Canadian country pop singer and songwriter. He has released two albums Good with Me (2020) and Family First (2024), and has two gold-certified singles in Canada with "California" and "If You Met Me First".[1][2][3]


Early life[edit]
Eric Ethridge was raised in Sarnia, Ontario and played football as a defensive end at the University of Western Ontario.[4] Prior to pursuing a full-time career in music, Ethridge attended a chiropractic school in Toronto and worked as a chiropractor in Sarnia.[5]

Career[edit]
Early career and self-titled EP[edit]
In 2016, he won the Canadian Country Music Association's Discovery Award.[5]
In July 2018, he released his self-titled debut EP Eric Ethridge.[6] The single "Liquor's Callin' the Shots" reached the top 30 of Canadian country radio.[5] In August 2018, he won the Grand Prize in the 2018 Unsigned Only Music Competition for his song "Girl On Fire".[7]
In November 2018, Ethridge signed a worldwide recording and publishing deal with ole, who placed him on their label, Red Dot.[8] Further singles from the EP included "Makin' Me Crazy",[5] "California",[9] and "If You Met Me First",[10] the latter two of which were certified Gold by Music Canada.[11]

2020-present: Good with Me[edit]
In February 2020, Ethridge released his debut US single "Dream Girl", co-written by Dan + Shay.[12][13] The track was then included on his April 2020 extended play Forever With You.[14][15]
In August 2020, Ethridge released the single "Kiss Me Goodbye"[16] On October 30, 2020, Ethridge released his debut full-length album Good with Me on Anthem Records (rebranded from Red Dot),[17][18] which includes
the singles "Dream Girl", "Kiss Me Goodbye",[2][19] and "Sad Songs".[20]
In 2022, Ethridge signed a management deal with Workshop Music Group and independently released the single "Made in Mexico".[21]

Personal life[edit]
Ethridge married fellow Canadian country singer-songwriter Kalsey Kulyk in December 2019.[22] During the COVID-19 pandemic, the couple moved from Nashville, Tennessee into a van and performed under the "Love on the Road" banner throughout Canada.[23][24] In August 2021, Kulyk gave birth to the couple's first child, a son named Wilder, in Alberta.[25]
